# Incident Review Profile

This public profile is for source-grounded incident reviews where the decision is how to explain what happened, what evidence is strongest, what remains unresolved, and what follow-up would prevent overclaiming.

## Project

Incident Review is a reusable profile for public technical, operational, historical, or policy incidents. It assumes the source corpus is assembled by the project owner before lane work starts.

## Decision Owner And Audience

- Decision owner: review lead
- Default audience: operations, product, engineering, risk, or editorial leadership
- Durable context destination: project knowledge base or durable research context

## Decision Taxonomy

Use this taxonomy unless a run brief narrows it:

1. Timeline decision
2. Causal-chain decision
3. Responsibility and scope decision
4. Recovery or mitigation decision
5. Counterevidence decision
6. Follow-up source collection decision
7. Public-claim readiness decision

If a research idea does not change one of these decisions, it waits.

## Default Lane Families

- Timeline reconciliation
- Primary-source causal chain
- Recovery actions and mitigation
- Counterevidence and alternate explanations
- Negative evidence and unresolved gaps

## Source Rules

- Prefer primary documents, official reports, transcripts, logs, direct statements, and public records.
- Treat secondary summaries as orientation unless the brief explicitly accepts them as evidence.
- Capture contradictions instead of flattening them.
- Do not infer motive, liability, or fault beyond cited evidence.

## Internal Context Rule

Profile docs are context and hypotheses, not external proof. Final claims still require source IDs from raw lane evidence.
