# Source Priority

## Default Priority

1. Official App Store and Google Play listings.
2. Competitor-owned pages: pricing, onboarding, help docs, privacy, risk disclosures, AI disclosures, and screenshots.
3. Public user reviews or comments available through allowed public inspection.
4. Search result pages and keyword suggestions from allowed public methods.
5. Secondary articles or app-list posts only after primary/user sources.

## Source Rules

- Treat marketplace metadata and screenshots as strong evidence for positioning, claims, categories, and visible feature gates.
- Treat reviews as evidence of user language and objections, not as statistically representative sentiment unless sample/method is explicit.
- Treat rankings, download estimates, revenue estimates, and keyword volume as weak unless the source provides methodology.
- Prefer official APIs or platform-owned pages before any extractor or scraper.
- Record blocked sources when access requires login, captcha bypass, paywall bypass, LinkedIn scraping, mass social scraping, or unapproved paid tooling.

## Negative Evidence

Record when a competitor lacks visible pricing, trust language, privacy claims, policy boundaries, risk language, screenshots, or review signals that the lane expected to find.
