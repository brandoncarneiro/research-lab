## 1. Executive Read

Inside the fictional corpus, chore coordination looks less like a missing task-list problem and more like a fairness, rotation, and reminder-tone problem. The strongest synthetic signal is that roommates dislike becoming the person who reminds everyone, while manual substitutes decay after initial setup (SYN-S1, SYN-S2, SYN-S3). The useful prototype shape is narrow: recurring chore assignment, visible rotation, and gentle reminders. The research does not support real market demand, pricing, launch, or category claims because every source is synthetic.

## 4. Signal Read

The fictional evidence supports a prototype hypothesis, not a business conclusion. The product job that survives synthesis is "help shared households maintain chore ownership and rotation without making one roommate the enforcer" (P1, P2). The main counter-signal is that chores may be only one slice of broader household coordination pain, and generic task tools can already represent recurring chores (N1, N2). The design risk is reminder tone: automation may help follow-through, but it can also feel patronizing or controlling (C1).
